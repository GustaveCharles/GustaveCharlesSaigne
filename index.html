Research
I'm broadly interested in security for (embodied) AI systems and modern cryptography.
Backdoors in Fine-tuned Robot AI Models
ETH Robotics Club  ·  2026 – present
When someone downloads a pre-trained AI model for a robot and fine-tunes it on their own data, could that model already contain a hidden backdoor that wakes up during fine-tuning? We're adapting this threat model from language models to the robotics setting, where a triggered backdoor has real physical consequences.
Attacking and Defending Robot AI at Inference Time
ETH Robotics Club  ·  2026 – present
We're stress-testing embodied AI models that control robots by feeding them inference-time adversarial inputs and observing what the model sees, how it understands instructions, and what actions it outputs. We're also building defenses inspired by prompt-injection-prevention techniques from AI security, applied to the robotics pipeline.
zkFHE
COMPSEC Lab, EPFL  ·  Feb 2025 – Dec 2025  ·  Supervisor: Christian Knabenhans
Designed and built a fast, modular library for creating cryptographic proofs to ensure a FHE computation was done correctly. Started as a semester project and grew into a full implementation and research paper, in collaboration with Prof. Chiesa's group.
Post-Quantum Proof Systems at Nethermind
Nethermind  ·  Sept 2024 – Dec 2024  ·  Cryptography Research Intern
Contributed to the Rust implementation of LatticeFold, a proof system built on lattice math that remains secure against quantum computers. Participated in the research effort to implement Falcon, a post-quantum signature scheme.
